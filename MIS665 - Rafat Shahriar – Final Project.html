Data¶

Data Source: The dataset is obtained from Cortez et al., 2009. It contains physicochemical variables like fixed acidity, volatile acidity, etc., and sensory variable quality. (Source URL: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/uciml/red-wine-quality-cortez-et-al-2009/code?datasetId=4458)

Variables:

  1. Fixed Acidity (Numeric): Represents the fixed (non-volatile) acids in the wine.
  2. Volatile Acidity (Numeric): Indicates the amount of acetic acid in the wine, which can contribute to an unpleasant vinegar taste.
  3. Citric Acid (Numeric):Represents the amount of citric acid in the wine, contributing to freshness and flavor.
  4. Residual Sugar (Numeric):Represents the amount of sugar remaining after fermentation, influencing sweetness.
  5. Chlorides (Numeric):Indicates the amount of salt in the wine, affecting taste and stability.
  6. Free Sulfur Dioxide (Numeric):Represents the free form of sulfur dioxide, which prevents microbial growth and oxidation.
  7. Total Sulfur Dioxide (Numeric):Represents the total amount of sulfur dioxide, including both free and bound forms.
  8. Density (Numeric):Represents the density of the wine, related to alcohol content and sweetness.
  9. pH (Numeric):Represents the acidity or basicity of the wine.
  10. Sulphates (Numeric): Represents the amount of sulfur dioxide in the wine, contributing to antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
  11. Alcohol (Numeric):Represents the alcohol content of the wine.
  12. Quality (Numeric):Represents the sensory output variable, indicating the quality of the wine.

X = wine.drop("quality", axis=1)
y = wine["quality"]

test_sizes = [0.5, 0.4, 0.3, 0.25, 0.2]

regressors = {
    "Decision Tree": DecisionTreeRegressor(),
    "Random Forest": RandomForestRegressor(),
    "Linear Regression": LinearRegression(),
    "K-Nearest Neighbors": KNeighborsRegressor(),
    "Support Vector Machine": SVR(),
}

for test_size in test_sizes:
    X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test = train_test_split(X, y, test_size=test_size, random_state=0)
    
    for reg_name, regressor in regressors.items():
        model = regressor.fit(X_train, y_train)
        pred_y = model.predict(X_test)

        mse = mean_squared_error(y_test, pred_y)
        rmse = np.sqrt(mse)
        r_squared = r2_score(y_test, pred_y)

        print(f"For {test_size:.0%} testing data (significant variables) using {reg_name}:")
        print(f"  Mean Squared Error: {mse:.4f}")
        print(f"  Root Mean Squared Error: {rmse:.4f}")
        print(f"  Mean Absolute Error: {mean_absolute_error(y_test, pred_y):.4f}")
        print(f"  R-squared: {r_squared:.4f}")
        print("------------------------------------------------------")
